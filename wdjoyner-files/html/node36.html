Derivative of a function of one variable

The fundamental definition of the Differential Calculus is:

Definition 4.4.1   The derivative4.4 of a function is the limit of the ratio of the increment of the function to the increment of the independent variable, when the latter increment varies and approaches the limit zero.

When the limit of this ratio exists, the function is said to be differentiable, or to possess a derivative.
