Application: Newton's method

6.12

Newton's method (also known as the Newtonâ€“Raphson method) is an efficient algorithm for finding approximations to the zeros (or roots) of a real-valued function. As such, it is an example of a root-finding algorithm. It produces iteratively a sequence of approximations to the root. It can also be used to find a minimum or maximum of such a function, by finding a zero in the function's first derivative.
